01 · Overview

Bluetooth wasn't built for this many devices.

Today's phones connect to one or two things well. Everything past that means manual switching, dropped audio, and unpredictable latency. MultiLink replaces that with a single orchestration layer built for a world where every person carries five to ten Bluetooth devices.

The Problem

  • Limited simultaneous audio connections
  • Manual, disruptive device switching
  • No intelligent prioritization
  • Bandwidth conflicts between devices
  • Rising latency as devices are added

The Solution

  • Simultaneous multi-device connectivity
  • AI-driven priority and scheduling
  • Dynamic, real-time bandwidth allocation
  • Context-aware automation by activity
  • One dashboard for every connected device
02 · Architecture

One engine, eleven layers, zero manual switching.

Each layer owns exactly one job — which is what lets MultiLink stay predictable as more devices, and future Bluetooth standards, are added.
